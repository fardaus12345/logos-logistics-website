# Logos Logistics Distribution Website System

## Strategic Foundation

This implementation uses the full `Home.docx` SEO content and preserves all supplied headings, service descriptions, process copy, location copy, industry copy, testimonial placeholder, quote copy, and FAQs. The content is arranged into a premium logistics design system instead of being shortened.

The site uses the official brand palette:

- Primary Logistics Blue: `#0A3D91`
- Accent Distribution Red: `#E63B3B`
- Deep Enterprise Navy: `#07182F`
- Industrial Slate: `#5E6B7A`
- Soft Industrial Background: `#F4F7FA`
- Clean White: `#FFFFFF`
- Optional Technology Accent: `#2F80ED`

## Reference Usage

- NFI Industries inspired the enterprise-scale hero, proof rhythm, and trust-first presentation.
- ODW Logistics inspired the service architecture, quote path, and structured 3PL language.
- Ryder inspired the enterprise CTA treatment and procurement-friendly page flow.
- ITS Logistics inspired the modern industrial KPI band, strong service pillars, and operational confidence.
- Kenco and WSI inspired the readable B2B service cards and practical internal-page structure.
- GXO inspired restrained enterprise motion and technology-enabled positioning.
- ShipBob and ShipMonk inspired the quote funnel, fulfillment conversion flow, and SaaS clarity.
- Stord, Redwood, project44, and FourKites inspired the dashboard-style EDI/API and operations visibility module.
- The mobile reference folder informed the stacked hero, collapsible nav, full-width mobile CTAs, and single-column service cards.

## Logo Influence

The current Logos brand mark was downloaded from the live website and used locally. The layout gives the logo a clear white navigation field, enough horizontal space, and no competing background behind it. The blue/red logo directly informed the CTA system: blue for primary action and red for active/hover emphasis.

## Responsive Behavior

- Full service rail on desktop/laptop
- Hamburger menu under enterprise breakpoint
- Single-column service and content sections on mobile
- Full-width CTAs on mobile
- Hero proof chips become a two-column mobile grid
- Timeline cards stack cleanly
- Forms remain readable and touch-friendly

## Motion Direction

Motion is intentionally light:

- Scroll reveal for sections
- Hover lift on service and related cards
- CTA hover transition from logistics blue to distribution red
- Mobile menu toggling
- FAQ disclosure behavior

This follows the research package guidance: smooth, premium, minimal, and operationally quiet.
